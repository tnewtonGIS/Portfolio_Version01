About

Serve as a member of a highly functioning team of Rangers alongside fellow General Rangers, Protection Rangers, other park staff, personnel from cooperating agencies, and volunteers. Use computers to communicate electronically; use radios to communicate daily. Conduct patrols throughout the park's backcountry trail network on foot and throughout the park's front country and fire roads via motor vehicle. Educate Park visitors about a variety of subjects, including recreational opportunities, natural and cultural history of the park and its resources, hazards and how to avoid them, backcountry safety, preparedness, safe hiking, and what to do in the case of an emergency while on patrol and while staged at busy trailheads. Deliver Preventative Search and Rescue (PSAR) messaging to visitors. Communicate in-person via impromptu contacts, as well as via informal and semi-formal presentations. Respond to emergency medical calls as an EMT, such as motor vehicle crashes, litter carryout rescues, helicopter rescues; drive the park ambulance (if trained); conduct hasty ground search operations; conduct visitor assists, such as lockouts, jump starts, and courtesy transports. Assist with managing Division equipment, such as rescue gear, medical supplies, office equipment, etc.; and facilities to include an ambulance bay, equipment caches, the park ambulance, and patrol vehicles.

Key Tasks

  • Daily backcountry patrols to check trail conditions.
  • Provide education using the PREPARE methodology coupled with the Ten Essentials.
  • Respond to lost and injured hikers to lead or carry individuals to safety.
  • Assist law enforcement with traffic accidents and storm damage throughout the park.

Pros and Cons

Pros

A bad rainy hurricane day in a National Park is better than a good day in a cubicle. Shenandoah's proximity to D.C. allows you to meet a wide range of high-level corporate leaders and senior executives, down to a plumber. Knowing the trail condition makes a huge difference in directing people to hikes more suited to a person's abilities. The Preventative piece of SAR, done correctly, can save parks hundreds of thousands of dollars in future recovery costs. You can buy yourself an Airstream and live in the park, listening to birds chirping and deer outside your door daily.

Cons

If you are an introvert, PSAR is not a job for you. Being a people person is imperative to delivering the PSAR message. Since no national PSAR standard exists, every park may or may not have health physicals or fitness standards, significantly impacting a ranger's capability. DOI agencies are like businesses. Some agencies sell mineral, timber, grazing rights, or electrical power. The Park Service sells enjoyment, which generates a small budget. NPS must adhere to the Historic Preservation Act for all buildings resulting in minimal to no modifications. The low GS/WG payscale and seasonal months make these jobs only sustainable for retired and college students.

About

Technical Consultants in Esri's Professional Services Division play a crucial role in the innovation and implementation of ArcGIS enterprise technology. As a Technical Consultant, I provided vision and leadership while promoting the use of Esri technology to enable customer success. We impact the development and growth of Esri technology into new domains by working closely with industry experts throughout the company. Evaluate customer requirements to decide the proper analysis tools for the problem. User needs include but are not limited to installing and configuring applications, automating workflows with model builder, writing custom Python or R scripts, designing GIS curricula and workshops, and supporting customer engagements at conferences. My federal customers include ASPRS, BIA, DOJ, FEMA, HHS OIG, IHS, and VHA. At the state level, I support a combination of Emergency Management offices, Police Departments, and Departments of Public Health for the states of Florida, Georgia, Maryland, Massachusetts, Oregon, and Virginia. Configuration work revolves around the Esri technologies suite of AGOL, ArcGIS Pro, Enterprise, Experience Builder, Fieldmaps, Portal, Sites/Hub, StoryMaps, Survey123, and Connect, Tracker, and Web Application Builder.

Key Tasks

  • Establish customer relations and collaborate desires to meet GIS needs.
  • Design, research, implement, and maintain geo-applications.
  • Programming and scripting complex desktop and web geospatial tools.
  • Prepare technical documents and GIS courses for a non-technical audience.

Pros and Cons

Pros

You will not find a more concentrated workforce of professional geo-centric scientists who are passionate about everything maps. The exposure to all of Esri's products, internal courses, and inside knowledge are shared to make you a professional in your area of focus. You will never be saddled with external distractions from creating maps and apps. You jump from one project to the next, taking the skills learned to make a better product for the next customer. Every position and team is different at Esri, but I was fortunate to have awesome bosses and colleagues with whom I could collaborate remotely and in the office. To sum Esri up, it's the `mappiest` place on earth.

Cons

All Esri offices are in high-cost-of-living cities, so you need to do a cost analysis to see if the juice is worth the squeeze. While Esri does have conferences, you have to work at Esri for a while before you can attend, even if the conference is within driving distance. Before covid, Esri employees would work with on-site customers to teach or establish GIS systems. Post covid, government agencies and civilian companies moved to remote conference. Can you handle a four-walled cubicle for the rest of your life?

About

As part of NASA’s Applied Sciences Program, DEVELOP addresses environmental and policy concerns by practically applying NASA Earth observations. DEVELOP projects apply Earth observations to the application areas of Agriculture, Disasters, Ecological Forecasting, Energy, Health and Air Quality, Transportation and infrastructure, urban development, and Water Resources. These projects highlight NASA Earth observation capabilities relative to environmental issues and concerns for enhanced policy and decision-making to improve life here on Earth. Our development team integrated Google Earth Image Landsat7 and Sentinel imagery with Esri Model Builder to determine winter decline signals over twenty years across four states. The team concentrated on Hemlock detection as these evergreen species are more fire resilient and improve stream quality. Our team successfully developed models to find hemlock locations and delivered training tutorials to allow the Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians to expand their GIS program and enhance their cultural awareness programs.

Key Tasks

  • Meet with stakeholders to discover research topics beneficial to the tribe.
  • Research and collect historical raster and vector data for time series analysis.
  • Create GIS models to transform, extract, transform, and load (ETL) to begin machine learning detection of tree species.
  • Create technical documentation and prepare briefings for natural resources and NASA teams.

Pros and Cons

Pros

NASA DEVELOP is a paid internship that allows students to collaborate with mentors who are federal scientists at the Ph.D. level. Our excellent mentors provided thoughtful ideas for directing our research and working with student scientists and tribal members to research real-world problems. You get a NASA stamp on your resume.

Cons

My cons are the circumstances resulting from covid. All 2020 teams were moved to remote work at the last minute and did not experience in-person collaboration. The two-day live training in R programming that we received as part of the program came so fast and furious it provided no value. If you already have an extensive programming language or know R, then this training can be applied to your research. Would I do this again remotely? Then NO; if I did it in person, then YES.

About

The mission of Global Treks focuses on experiential education opportunities for students wishing to expand their learning outside of the classroom. The Global Treks Internship Program was launched to provide students with a 3 ½ month experience combining action research, field data collection, and concluding with the publication writing process. The internship opportunity connects each student's academic journey to a research project for publication. All research collected is professionally written and published in a cultural guide or field guide to a remote part of the world. The 2019 Rarotonga Expedition land trek continues to build sustainable relationships with diverse communities around the island. As the sole Geospatial Intern representative, you are responsible for items related to collection, interpretation, analysis, and product delivery to publication. You will also be assigned additional duties for UAV imagery and video collection for short movie clips. Devise a way to map lagoon imagery with or without GRS panels accurately. You must ensure QA/QC standards match Cook Island collection data to the correct grid coordinate reference and projection systems while representing spatially accurate 3d enhanced imagery and contour profiles.

Key Tasks

  • Collect trail tracks with multiple GPS units and important plot island features such as water points and bus stops
  • Aerial flight to collect, and produce raster coastal imagery
  • Research and collect NZ raster DEM and SHP files for map information
  • Produce printable maps, gdb files, and book-ready map layouts.

Pros and Cons

Pros

You're visiting a tropical paradise, hiking, snorkeling, and scuba diving, and exploring the country. Then, apply your education specialty to production products. Great food and friendly islanders are welcoming. I recommend arriving early to take the scooter driver's license, which allows for pre/post data collection as you may need more time (also a great collector item).

Cons

GTA is an unpaid internship. You pay the cost, but remember that this is a working vacation. Some people pay for ecotourism, but here, you get to work with a team of like-minded individuals publishing a book. If you need more funds for a GTA internship, talk with your professors and see if there is any department money to help.

About

As the largest United States military branch, we defend our nation's freedom and safety and provide stability during challenging times. Freedom has been a focus since 1775, when the Army's fight for independence began. Today, the Army honors a legacy of freedom and leads by example with core values that every Soldier lives by. As a soldier, I trained in various military-related tasks, including administrative, combat, communications, humanitarian functions, leadership, medical, peacekeeping duties, and teaching forces around the globe. While serving as a soldier, I lived the Army's core values of Loyalty, Duty, Respect, Selfless Service, Honor, Integrity, and Personal Courage. Today, the Army employs over 450,000 active duty soldiers, ready to protect America.

Key Tasks

  • Reads, interprets, and employs maps for GEOINT operations
  • Leads by, with, and through military forces as an advisor
  • Establishes communication arrays to meet mission requirements and provides secondary medical triage
  • Prepares excellent MRE rations and combat-ready canteen cups to improve soldier moral

Pros and Cons

Pros

No matter what military occupation you choose, rest assured that there is no better job or career for today's youth. Acedmia is not for everyone straight out of high school. The Army gives you a chance to grow up, learn new skills, receive a pay check, free room-board and food, and a paid four-year college education once you leave. You get a chance to leave your small town to see the world. Those who are doing short-term work also leave now with a 401k equivalent in lieu of having to serve 20 years for a pension. If you stay to retirement, Tricare and Veteran Health Administration will see to your healthcare for life.

Cons

Given the American youth's culture today, the military can be physically and emotionally challenging. However, the Army of 2020s is a more intellectual force vs. a physical Army. The days of Billy Waugh or Mad Dog Shirver are long gone. Soldiers are expected to behave in a "Boy Scout" manner with almost a zero-tolerance policy for misconduct. The policy is an ongoing national security issue, as roughneck men 'play hard - work hard,' combined with men already disqualified by health problems or mental health issues, have limited eligible recruits. Those looking for a stable family life will often be interrupted by deployments in various lengths. Plan to miss many birthdays and holidays at the cost of the greater good.
